Care & Maintenance

Proper care ensures your LUNVYR fixtures maintain their beauty and performance for decades. Follow our comprehensive guide for finish-specific maintenance.

Finish Care

Care by Finish Type

Natural Brass

Living finish that develops patina over time

  • Dust weekly with microfiber cloth
  • Clean with warm water and mild soap
  • Dry immediately to prevent water spots
  • Apply brass cleaner monthly for shine
  • Wax quarterly with paste wax
  • Embrace natural patina development

Aged Brass

Pre-patinated finish with rich depth

  • Dust regularly with soft brush
  • Clean gently with damp cloth only
  • Avoid brass cleaners and polishes
  • Pat dry, never rub aggressively
  • Protect patina with light wax coat
  • Touch up with patina solution if needed

Blackened Steel

Dark oxide finish requiring gentle care

  • Dust with soft, dry cloth
  • Use minimal moisture when cleaning
  • Apply light coat of paste wax
  • Avoid abrasive cleaners
  • Check for rust in humid areas
  • Re-blacken if finish wears

Polished Steel

High-gloss finish requiring regular maintenance

  • Clean weekly with stainless cleaner
  • Buff with microfiber for shine
  • Remove fingerprints immediately
  • Polish monthly with metal polish
  • Protect with clear coat if desired
  • Address scratches promptly

Best Practices

Do's and Don'ts

Do's

  • Turn off power before any maintenance
  • Use appropriate cleaning products for each finish
  • Clean glass components regularly for optimal light output
  • Check mounting hardware periodically
  • Store cleaning supplies in original containers
  • Follow manufacturer's care instructions
  • Document maintenance dates and procedures
  • Replace worn or damaged components promptly
  • Use soft brushes for detailed cleaning
  • Apply protective coatings as recommended

Don'ts

  • Never use abrasive cleaners on metal finishes
  • Don't use ammonia-based products on brass
  • Avoid pressure washing or high-pressure water
  • Don't disassemble electrical components
  • Never use steel wool or scouring pads
  • Don't allow water to pool on metal surfaces
  • Avoid cleaning when fixture is warm from use
  • Don't use generic metal polishes without testing
  • Never spray cleaners directly on electrical parts
  • Don't ignore signs of wear or damage

Problem Solving

Troubleshooting Guide

Finish appears dull or cloudy

Cause: Buildup of oils, dust, or improper cleaning products.

Solution: Clean with appropriate finish-specific cleaner, rinse thoroughly, and dry completely. Apply protective coating if recommended for your finish type.

Water spots on metal surface

Cause: Mineral deposits from hard water or incomplete drying.

Solution: Use a 50/50 vinegar and water solution to dissolve mineral deposits. Rinse with clean water and dry immediately. Consider using distilled water for cleaning in hard water areas.

LED lights flickering or dim

Cause: Incompatible dimmer, loose connections, or LED driver issues.

Solution: Check dimmer compatibility first. Verify all electrical connections are secure. If problem persists, contact a licensed electrician to inspect the LED driver and connections.

Scratches on polished surfaces

Cause: Abrasive cleaning materials or accidental contact with sharp objects.

Solution: Minor scratches can be buffed out with appropriate metal polish. Deep scratches may require professional refinishing. Always use soft cloths and non-abrasive cleaners.

Fixture feels loose or wobbly

Cause: Loose mounting hardware or insufficient ceiling box support.

Solution: Turn off power and check all mounting screws and connections. If ceiling box appears inadequate, consult an electrician about upgrading to a properly rated mounting system.

Glass appears cloudy or etched

Cause: Hard water deposits, chemical etching from cleaners, or age-related deterioration.

Solution: Try cleaning with white vinegar solution. If etching has occurred, the glass may need replacement. Use only glass-safe cleaners and ensure complete rinsing.
